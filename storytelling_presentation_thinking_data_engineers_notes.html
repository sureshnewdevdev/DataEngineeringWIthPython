Classroom Opening

1. Learning Objective

Data engineers often focus on pipelines, code, schemas, and performance. But stakeholders care about business meaning, risks, impact, timelines, and decisions. Storytelling helps convert technical findings into stakeholder-ready communication.

By the end of this session, learners will understand:

  • How to communicate technical data issues in business language.
  • How to explain source-system issues clearly.
  • How to present pipeline logic without overloading stakeholders.
  • How to define and explain data contracts.
  • How to connect data engineering work to business impact.
  • How to prepare the Outcome of Step 2 after exploratory analysis.
Trainer note: Tell learners: “A good data engineer does not only build pipelines. A good data engineer helps stakeholders understand what the data means and what decisions are needed.”
Core Concept

2. Why Storytelling Matters for Data Engineers

Data engineering storytelling is the ability to explain data problems, pipeline decisions, risks, and outcomes in a way that business and technical stakeholders can understand.

Clarity

Converts technical findings into simple messages.

Trust

Shows data quality issues transparently.

Decision Support

Helps stakeholders approve rules and priorities.

Business Value

Connects pipeline work to reports, KPIs, and outcomes.

Interview answer: Storytelling for data engineers means explaining technical findings such as schema issues, data quality problems, pipeline logic, and data contracts in business-friendly language so stakeholders can make informed decisions.

5. How to Communicate Source-System Issues

Source-system issues should be communicated with facts, examples, and business impact. Avoid blaming the source team. Focus on data behavior and pipeline impact.

IssueTechnical ExplanationStakeholder-Friendly ExplanationDecision Needed
Missing CustomerIdCustomerId has 12% nulls.Some sales records cannot be linked to customers.Load as Unknown or reject?
Schema DriftNew column added in source file.Source format changed and may break pipeline.Should new column be added to contract?
Duplicate OrderIdOrderId appears multiple times.Sales may be counted twice in reports.Which record should be retained?
Invalid AmountAmount has negative values.Revenue may be understated or incorrect.Are negative values refunds or errors?

Example Communication

Finding:
12% of sales records have missing CustomerId.

Business Meaning:
These records cannot be linked to customer-level dashboards.

Risk:
Customer revenue, repeat purchase, and churn analysis may be incomplete.

Decision Needed:
Should we reject these records, map them to Unknown Customer, or ask source team to correct them?

Recommended Action:
Load them into an exception table and continue processing valid records.
Topic 2

6. How to Explain Pipeline Logic

Stakeholders do not need every line of code. They need to understand what the pipeline does, what rules are applied, what data is rejected, and how the final output is produced.

Pipeline StepTechnical LogicBusiness Explanation
IngestRead sales CSV from SFTP.Collect daily sales data from store system.
ValidateCheck required columns and data types.Ensure required business fields are available.
CleanRemove duplicates and invalid amounts.Prevent double-counting and incorrect revenue.
TransformCalculate tax and net sales.Prepare finance-ready sales metrics.
LoadWrite Gold table.Make data available for Power BI dashboard.
Trainer explanation: Explain pipeline logic as business rules, not only technical operations.
Topic 3

7. How to Communicate Data Contracts

A data contract is an agreement between data producers and consumers. It defines expected schema, data types, required fields, quality rules, freshness, and ownership.

Contract AreaExampleWhy It Matters
SchemaOrderId, CustomerId, OrderDate, AmountPrevents pipeline breakage.
Data TypeAmount must be decimal.Prevents calculation errors.
Required FieldsOrderId and Amount are mandatory.Ensures minimum reporting quality.
FreshnessFile must arrive before 1 AM daily.Ensures dashboard refresh.
OwnershipSales system team owns source quality.Clarifies responsibility.

Sample Data Contract Summary

Dataset: Daily Sales File
Owner: Sales System Team
Consumer: Data Engineering and BI Team
Frequency: Daily
Expected Arrival: Before 1 AM
Mandatory Columns: OrderId, CustomerId, OrderDate, Amount
Quality Rules:
- OrderId should be unique
- Amount should be greater than or equal to 0
- OrderDate should be valid
Action on Failure:
- Critical issue: stop pipeline
- Non-critical issue: move to exception table
Topic 4

8. How to Communicate Business Impact

Every technical issue should be connected to business impact. This helps stakeholders understand priority and urgency.

Technical IssuePossible Business ImpactPriority
Duplicate ordersRevenue may be over-reported.High
Missing customer IDsCustomer analytics may be incomplete.Medium
Delayed source fileDashboard refresh may be delayed.High
Invalid product codesCategory-level sales may be incorrect.Medium
Schema driftPipeline may fail or ignore new data.High
Best practice: Present technical issue + business risk + recommended action + owner + deadline.

Step 2 Output Template

Step 2 Outcome: Source Data Understanding Summary

Dataset:
Daily Sales File

Key Findings:
1. OrderId is unique for 97.8% of records.
2. CustomerId is missing in 12% of records.
3. Amount has negative values in 1.5% of records.
4. OrderDate has multiple date formats.

Business Impact:
- Missing CustomerId affects customer analytics.
- Negative Amount may represent refunds or source errors.
- Multiple date formats may delay pipeline processing.

Recommended Pipeline Rules:
- Validate mandatory columns before processing.
- Move missing CustomerId records to exception table.
- Confirm negative Amount business meaning with Finance.
- Standardize OrderDate during Silver transformation.

Stakeholder Decisions Needed:
- Should missing CustomerId records be rejected or loaded as Unknown?
- Are negative Amount records valid refund transactions?
- Should new source columns be accepted automatically?

10. Presentation Templates for Data Engineers

One-Slide Summary Format

Title:
Source Data Assessment - Daily Sales

What We Analyzed:
Daily sales file from POS system.

What We Found:
- 12% missing CustomerId
- Duplicate OrderId in 2.2% records
- Amount column includes negative values
- OrderDate has inconsistent formats

Why It Matters:
These issues can impact revenue reporting and customer analytics.

Recommended Action:
Add validation rules, exception handling, and data contract approval.

Decision Needed:
Confirm handling rule for missing CustomerId and negative Amount.

Executive-Friendly Explanation

The data pipeline can be built, but source data quality issues must be resolved or controlled.
The biggest risks are missing customer identifiers and inconsistent date formats.
If not handled, dashboards may show incomplete customer-level reporting.
We recommend adding validation rules and creating an exception report for business review.
